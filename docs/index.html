About my research

I want to know what makes "good UX".

In my research, I try to find out how to incorporate enriching aesthetics beyond acceptable optics into the development of assistive technology.​
Another focus of my work are various quality aspects and metrics of user experience, i.e. what constitutes UX and how to measure it. I am also particularly curious about how users' motivations and emotions influence their experiences. My multi-disciplinary background heavily inspires my research approach and oftentimes results in interdisciplinary references and ways of thinking. ​Providing theoretically sound rationales for my studies is a central goal for me.

Currently, I am a PhD researcher at the research group for HCI & Accessibility at Karlsruhe Institute for Technology (KIT), Germany.

About me

Above all, I love to learn.

My degrees include a Bachelor of Arts in Theater Studies, Philosophy, and Dutch Language and Literature from the Free University of Berlin as well as a Bachelor of Science and a Master of Science in Computer Science and Digital Media with a focus on HCI from the University of Applied Sciences Emden/Leer.
I have been granted an award for the best Bachelor's degree in my faculty as well as multiple scholarships for excellence in my studies.

In the summer semester of 2022, I worked as a lecturer in Media Science & Media Critique. I am also a skilled product owner & project manager in web development and have gained work experience in both agencies and the public sector. Besides that, I have professional knowledge in the production and PR of cultural events and organizations.

As I am passionate about social justice, I direct some of my time to this: Currently, I am mentoring at Arbeiterkind.de, an organization that supports first-gen university students, and Jugend hackt, a programme that encourages kids to develop technology for good. I have also been volunteering with the NGOs Sabalamby Unnayan Samity and Netz Bangladesh in the areas of human rights, non-formal primary education, and basic needs of the ultra poor.

Hamburg, Germany, dubbed the most beautiful city in the world, is my adoptive hometown. In my freetime, I watch plays at the theatre, visit concerts, and spend time with a 38%-dachshund named Hilde.
